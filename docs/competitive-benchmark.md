## Deep dive: 5 best-in-class references

### 1. Pitching Performance Lab (PPL) — Kentucky

- **IA:** Home → How PPL Helps → Why Athletes Choose → What Parents Say → Our Training Process (5 steps) → Training Options (membership tiers) → FAQ → Footer (About, Team, Locations, Contact, Support, Legal).
- **CTA patterns:** Primary: “Build My Plan” (phone/location) and “Become A PPL Athlete” (registration). Secondary: “PICK YOUR TRAINING Membership HERE”; “PPL Youth” for ages 9–12. One clear next step for serious pitchers.
- **Program discovery:** Process is linear (Movement Screening → Assessment Bullpen → Review → Custom Program → Individualized Coaching). Membership cards: 1x/Week, Remote, UNLIMITED Pitching, Unlimited Pitching + (with PT). Onboarding fee and commitment stated.
- **Coach/facility:** “Our Team” in footer; “Expert coaching” in hero; testimonials name athletes (Jake Faherty, Tyler Guilfoil, Austin Strickland) and teams (Marlins, Astros, Giants) plus parent names (Kevin Staton, Rob Krift, Randy Faherty).
- **Trust signals:** Video testimonials; “Trusted by competitive arms from Little League to MLB”; integrated PT; FAQ (age, velocity, command, mechanics, weighted balls, billing, parents, group setting).
- **Results framing:** Testimonials cite velo (e.g. 86–88 → 100 mph) and command/mental growth; “they prepare you” / “prepare players to take ownership.”

**Takeaway for Mine:** One hero CTA (“Book an evaluation” or “Get started”); clear 3–5 step process; athlete + parent testimonials with names/outcomes; FAQ; transparent pricing/commitment.

### 2. The Baseball Lab — Overland Park, KS

- **IA:** Home → Welcome (tech + programming) → Pitching Lab, Hitting Lab, Memberships, Lessons, Tunnel Rentals → Upcoming events/classes/camps → Become A Member → “Training backed by science” → Training Resources (Start Your Training, Become A Member) → Services list (Tunnels, Lessons, Hitting/Pitching programs, Assessments, Biomechanics, Models).
- **CTA patterns:** “Make A Reservation” and “Become A Member” above the fold; “Explore Member Benefits”; “Start Your Training” (classes); “Reserve your trial tunnel today” in copy.
- **Program discovery:** Clear buckets (Pitching Lab vs Hitting Lab vs Memberships vs Lessons vs Tunnel Rentals). Classes and camps linked to booking (RunSwift); event list with times and links.
- **Coach/facility:** “Lessons and Instructors” link; “experienced instructors”; “experts in biomechanics, athletic training, and data analytics”; location (153rd and Metcalf).
- **Trust signals:** “Data-driven”; “23 MLB teams” (iPitch); “state-of-the-art”; motion capture, Trackman; “youth, high school, college, and professional athletes.”
- **Results framing:** “Enhance training efficiency and improve performance”; “comprehensive athlete assessment” (speed, strength, agility, movement, body comp, biomechanics).

**Takeaway for Mine:** Dual CTA (book trial + join); clear program buckets; tech and data language; link events/classes to booking; member benefits spelled out.

### 3. Driveline Baseball — multi-location

- **IA:** (From search/site structure) Home, Locations (WA, AZ, FL), Training (youth, camps, gym), Academy (youth teams), research/technology. Strong emphasis on outcomes and facility scale.
- **CTA patterns:** Likely “Get started” / “Book” / “Join” at flagship; location-specific CTAs for each gym.
- **Program discovery:** Youth (9U–18U), camps, gym-based training; clear age bands and program types.
- **Coach/facility:** College/pro playing or coaching experience; 40K+ sq ft flagship; research-grade motion capture; multiple gyms.
- **Trust signals:** Brand authority; “gold-standard”; used by MLB to youth; stated outcomes (e.g. 5 mph throw, 7 mph exit velo academy-wide).
- **Results framing:** Velocity and exit velocity gains; “research-backed”; professional-level tech.

**Takeaway for Mine:** Outcome-focused copy (velo, exit velo) where accurate; age bands; “facility + tech” credibility; multi-location if applicable.

### 4. SCV Hitting Lab & Sports Performance — Santa Clarita, CA

- **IA:** Home centers on service grid with clear labels and prices: Adult Group Training, Pitching, Catching, Fielding, Hitting (baseball/softball), Speed and Agility, Sports Nutrition. “View all” CTA.
- **CTA patterns:** Each service has a price and presumably book/buy; single “View all” for discovery.
- **Program discovery:** By discipline (hitting, pitching, catching, fielding, speed, nutrition); price visible ($60–$150).
- **Coach/facility:** “Premiere” positioning; area (Santa Clarita); no deep coach bios on fetched page.
- **Trust signals:** Transparent pricing; multiple disciplines under one roof.
- **Results framing:** Less explicit; service-led.

**Takeaway for Mine:** Simple program grid with clear names and optional pricing; “View all” or “See programs” for discovery.

### 5. Texas Hit Lab — Leander, TX

- **IA:** (From search) Members-only; three 45x12 combo cages; membership tiers ($25 basic, $75 elite with session); simple entry path.
- **CTA patterns:** Join / membership focus; tiered plans.
- **Program discovery:** Basic vs Elite membership; one included 30-min session daily at elite.
- **Trust signals:** Members-only; clear pricing; structure (cages, sessions).
- **Results framing:** Not prominent on home.

**Takeaway for Mine:** If Mine offers memberships, tier names and one clear benefit per tier (e.g. sessions, access) help.
